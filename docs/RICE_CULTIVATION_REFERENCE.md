## Rice Varieties - Growth Stages and Activities

### NSIC Rc222 (Sinandomeng)
- **Maturity**: 106-114 days
- **Type**: Lowland rice variety
- **Source**: PhilRice Variety Information

#### Growth Stages (Based on PhilRice Guidelines)

1. **Pre-Planting Stage** (Days -21 to -1)
   - Land preparation
   - Seed bed preparation
   - Seed selection and treatment
   - Source: PhilRice Rice Production Manual, Land Preparation Module

2. **Germination/Seedling Stage** (Days 1-20)
   - Seed germination (3-5 days)
   - Seedling establishment (15-20 days)
   - Initial root and shoot development
   - Source: DA Rice Production Guide, Seedling Management

3. **Vegetative Stage** (Days 21-55)
   - Tillering phase
   - Leaf development
   - Root system expansion
   - Water management critical
   - Source: PhilRice Technical Bulletin on Vegetative Growth

4. **Reproductive Stage** (Days 56-85)
   - Panicle initiation
   - Panicle development
   - Flowering (heading)
   - Pollination and fertilization
   - Source: DA Rice Growth Stages Reference

5. **Ripening Stage** (Days 86-114)
   - Grain filling
   - Milk stage
   - Dough stage
   - Maturity
   - Source: PhilRice Harvest Management Guidelines

### NSIC Rc160 (Rc160)
- **Maturity**: 105-110 days
- **Type**: Lowland inbred rice
- **Source**: PhilRice Variety Database

Similar growth stage pattern as NSIC Rc222 with slight timing variations.

### PSB Rc18 (Oryza)
- **Maturity**: 115-120 days
- **Type**: Long-duration variety
- **Source**: PhilRice-Philippine Seed Board Variety Release

Longer vegetative and reproductive stages due to extended maturity period.

## Activity Scheduling by Stage

### Pre-Planting Activities
Based on PhilRice Rice Production Manual (2020 Edition)

1. **21 days before transplant**: Land preparation - first plowing
2. **14 days before**: Second plowing and harrowing
3. **7 days before**: Final land leveling
4. **5 days before**: Seed bed preparation
5. **3 days before**: Seed treatment and soaking

### Seedling Stage Activities
Source: DA Bureau of Plant Industry - Rice Production Guidelines

- **Day 1-5**: Monitor germination
- **Day 7**: First fertilizer application (nursery)
- **Day 15**: Seedling hardening
- **Day 20**: Transplanting readiness check

### Vegetative Stage Activities
Source: PhilRice Crop Management Recommendations

- **Day 21**: Transplanting (if transplanted method)
- **Day 28**: First weeding
- **Day 35**: Basal fertilizer application
- **Day 42**: Maintain water level (5cm)
- **Day 49**: Second weeding if needed

### Reproductive Stage Activities
Source: PhilRice Fertilizer Management Guide

- **Day 56**: Panicle initiation fertilizer
- **Day 63**: Increase water depth to 10cm
- **Day 70**: Monitor for pests (rice bug, stem borer)
- **Day 77**: Maintain continuous flooding
- **Day 84**: Pre-flowering water management

### Ripening Stage Activities
Source: PhilRice Post-Production Guidelines

- **Day 86-100**: Grain filling - maintain shallow flooding
- **Day 100-107**: Reduce water gradually
- **Day 107-114**: Drain field 7-10 days before harvest
- **Day 114+**: Harvest at 80-85% grain maturity

## NPK Management Guidelines

### Based on PhilRice Nutrient Management Strategy

#### Nitrogen (N)
- **Critical Level**: <20 mg/kg (severe deficiency)
- **Low Level**: 20-40 mg/kg (requires fertilization)
- **Optimal**: 40-60 mg/kg
- **Source**: PhilRice Soil Fertility Manual

#### Phosphorus (P)
- **Critical Level**: <10 mg/kg (severe deficiency)
- **Low Level**: 10-20 mg/kg (requires fertilization)
- **Optimal**: 20-30 mg/kg
- **Source**: DA Soil Analysis Standards

#### Potassium (K)
- **Critical Level**: <30 mg/kg (severe deficiency)
- **Low Level**: 30-60 mg/kg (requires fertilization)
- **Optimal**: 60-80 mg/kg
- **Source**: PhilRice Soil Testing Laboratory Standards

### Recommended Fertilizer Application Rates
Source: PhilRice Fertilizer Recommendations (2021)

**For Lowland Rice:**
- **Basal Application** (Day 0-7): 30-40 kg N/ha, 30 kg P2O5/ha, 30 kg K2O/ha
- **Active Tillering** (Day 21-30): 30-40 kg N/ha
- **Panicle Initiation** (Day 45-50): 30-40 kg N/ha, 30 kg K2O/ha

**Total per season**: 90-120 kg N/ha, 30 kg P2O5/ha, 60 kg K2O/ha

## Water Management

### Based on PhilRice Water Management Protocols

1. **Seedling Stage**: Saturated to 2cm depth
2. **Vegetative Stage**: 3-5cm continuous flooding
3. **Reproductive Stage**: 5-10cm continuous flooding
4. **Ripening Stage**: 
   - Early ripening: 2-5cm
   - Late ripening: Gradual drainage
   - Final 7-10 days: Complete drainage

**Source**: PhilRice Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD) Guide

## Pest and Disease Management

### Common Issues by Stage
Source: DA Plant Protection Division - Rice IPM Manual

#### Vegetative Stage
- **Stem borer**: Monitor at 25-35 days
- **Leaf folder**: Check weekly
- **Brown planthopper**: Begin monitoring at 30 days

#### Reproductive Stage
- **Rice bug**: Critical at flowering
- **Blast disease**: Monitor during heading
- **Bacterial blight**: Prevent with proper nitrogen management

#### Ripening Stage
- **Rice grain bug**: Most critical threat
- **Birds**: Implement bird scaring at 80% maturity

## Variety-Specific Considerations

### NSIC Rc222 (Sinandomeng)
**Source**: PhilRice Variety Sheet - NSIC Rc222

- **Optimal planting**: Wet season or irrigated dry season
- **Seed rate**: 40 kg/ha (transplanted), 100-120 kg/ha (direct seeded)
- **Spacing**: 20cm x 20cm (transplanted)
- **Yield potential**: 5-6 tons/ha
- **Grain quality**: Premium quality, good eating quality

**Special Notes**:
- Moderately resistant to blast and bacterial blight
- Susceptible to brown planthopper - monitor closely
- Responds well to balanced fertilization

### NSIC Rc160
**Source**: PhilRice Technical Bulletin Rc160

- **Maturity**: Earlier than Rc222 by 4-9 days
- **Yield**: Slightly higher (5.5-6.5 tons/ha)
- **Resistance**: Better brown planthopper resistance
- **Water requirement**: Moderate

### PSB Rc18 (Oryza)
**Source**: PhilRice-PSB Variety Recommendations

- **Maturity**: Longer duration (115-120 days)
- **Best for**: Irrigated lowland with good water control
- **Yield**: 5.0-6.0 tons/ha
- **Special care**: Requires consistent water management during reproductive stage

## Direct-Seeded vs Transplanted Methods

### Direct-Seeded (Dry)
**Source**: PhilRice Direct Seeding Technology Package

**Advantages**:
- Labor-saving (no seedling production)
- Faster establishment
- Lower cost

**Key Activities**:
- **Day -7**: Land preparation and leveling
- **Day -3**: Pre-germinate seeds
- **Day 0**: Direct seeding at 80-100 kg/ha
- **Day 7-14**: Early weed control critical
- **Day 21**: First top dressing

### Transplanted Method
**Source**: PhilRice Transplanting Guidelines

**Advantages**:
- Better weed control
- More uniform stand
- Traditional method

**Key Activities**:
- **Day -21**: Prepare seed bed
- **Day -18**: Sow in seed bed
- **Day 0 (Day 21 from sowing)**: Transplant seedlings
- **Day 7 after transplant**: Gap filling
- **Day 14**: First top dressing

## Climate and Seasonal Considerations

### Wet Season (June-November)
**Source**: DA-PhilRice Seasonal Rice Production Guide

- Higher disease and pest pressure
- More cloud cover affects photosynthesis
- Adjust fertilizer rates (reduce by 10-20%)
- Enhanced drainage needed

### Dry Season (December-May)
**Source**: PhilRice Dry Season Management

- Better grain quality potential
- Lower pest pressure
- Higher fertilizer efficiency
- Irrigation management critical
- Watch for water stress

## Technology Integration

### Smart Farming Recommendations
**Source**: PhilRice Digital Agriculture Program

1. **NPK Sensors**: 
   - Check weekly during vegetative stage
   - Bi-weekly during reproductive and ripening

2. **Water Level Monitoring**:
   - Maintain optimal depth per stage
   - Implement AWD (Alternate Wetting and Drying) to save water

3. **Weather Integration**:
   - Adjust activities based on forecasts
   - Delay fertilization during heavy rain

4. **Growth Stage Tracking**:
   - Monitor daily to adjust activities
   - Document actual vs. expected progress
